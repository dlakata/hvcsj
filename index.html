The HVCSJ is a non-profit organization, and here's our mission statement.

Mission Statement:

The purpose of the HVCSJ is to provide an opportunity for students from Grade Kindergarten to 12th grade to learn chess and to improve their skills in chess, and to compete in tournaments against other players.

Narrative Description:

In the past, the HVCSJ has provided chess instruction, chess drills, and US Chess Federation-rated chess tournaments to its members. The HVCSJ has also organized is members into teams to participate in local and regional USCF chess tournaments. The chess instruction had included inviting top chess masters to give lectures to our club members.

Currently, the HVCSJ continues to provide chess instruction, chess drills, and USCF rated tournaments to its players. In addition, to organizing its members into club teams, the current activities have been expanded to the point where the HVCSJ also organizes its members into teams by individual school in order to compete for school team trophies at local and regional USCF rated tournaments. Also, the HVCSJ now encourages its high school members to earn community and school volunteer hours by instructing other club members in the advanced skills of chess. The HVCSJ also participates in community fairs in which the HVCSJ operated an oversized chess set and invites members of the local community to learn about the game of chess.

The HVCSJ plans to continue all its current activities and to expand its activities by organizing chess matches against other clubs. In addition, the HVCSJ plans to monitor the location of national tournaments and to send its members to national tournaments whenever possible.

  • Trophy program: The HVCSJ provides goods in the form of trophies to all members at the end of each term as recognition for their participation in the club.
  • Uniform program: The HVCSJ provides goods in the form of team shirts to all members.
  • Instruction program: The HVCSJ provides services in the form of chess instruction to all its members.
  • Activities program: The HVCSJ provides services in the form of organized chess activities at each club meeting.
  • Tournament Entrance Fee program: The HVCSJ provides funds to its members in the form of paying the tournament entrance fee to all USCF tournaments sponsored by the club.
Year Event
1978 Joe Huttlemeyer founds the club at the Huntingdon Valley Library's former location
1979 Sam Vallerio becomes club's director
1990 Club begins running annual USCF-rated regional tournaments
1995 Sam Vallerio retires and John Keir became club's director
1996 Club begins meeting in the Huntingdon Valley Firehouse during relocation of the Huntingdon Valley Library
1997 Bruce Fenderson becomes club's director; the club begins meeting in the Huntingdon Valley Library's new location
1998 Robert Lakata becomes club's director
2002 The club becomes affiliated with the HVAA
2005 The club begins running the annual Philadelphia Metropolitan Scholastic Chess Championships at The Meadowbrook School
